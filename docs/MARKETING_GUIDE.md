# DevApply Marketing Guide

## Product Position

DevApply is a **developer job application tracker and career workflow
tool**.

The long-term vision is to evolve into a **Developer Career CRM**.

---

# Target Audience

Primary audience:

- software developers
- junior developers looking for first job
- mid-level developers managing job search
- developers applying to many companies

Secondary audience:

- bootcamp graduates
- career switchers
- freelance developers

---

# Core Problem

Developers often:

- track job applications in spreadsheets
- lose track of interviews
- forget follow-ups
- have no structured pipeline

DevApply solves this with a **clean workflow tool built specifically for
developers.**

---

# Positioning

DevApply =

Developer Job Tracker\

- Application Pipeline\
- Career CRM

Competitors:

- Notion templates
- spreadsheets
- generic job trackers

Advantage:

DevApply is **purpose-built for developers**.

---

# Growth Strategy

## Phase 1: Early traction

Focus on:

- indie developer communities
- Reddit
- Twitter/X dev community
- Hacker News

Content ideas:

- "How I track 100 job applications as a developer"
- "Developer job search workflow"
- "Best job tracker for developers"

---

# SEO Strategy

Target keywords:

- developer job tracker
- job application tracker for developers
- software engineer job tracker
- developer job pipeline

Write articles around:

- job search organization
- interview preparation
- application workflows

---

# Distribution Channels

Best channels:

- Reddit (r/webdev, r/cscareerquestions)
- Twitter/X developer community
- IndieHackers
- Hacker News

---

# Pricing Strategy

Initial model:

Free tier:

- basic job tracking
- limited entries

Paid tier:

- analytics
- resume management
- automation
- unlimited tracking

Target price:

\$5--\$12/month

Goal:

Reach **\$500 MRR early with \~50--100 users.**

---

# Marketing Principles

- focus on developer audience
- avoid generic SaaS messaging
- show real product screenshots
- keep messaging simple and practical
